Getting started

Design principles

Six principles set the tone for every screen. They favour trust and clarity over decoration, and treat accessibility and Arabic RTL as baseline requirements rather than enhancements.

The six principles

🛡️
Trust over flash

Calm, official, restrained. No decorative noise, no marketing gloss.

🎯
Service-first

Every screen makes the task and the next step obvious. Outcomes are explicit.

♿
Accessibility-first

Accessible by default, not as a later pass.

🔁
Arabic RTL correctness

A baseline requirement, not an enhancement.

🧩
Consistency over novelty

Reuse patterns; do not invent one-off visual treatments.

📐
Clear hierarchy

One primary action per view; strong, legible typographic hierarchy.

Applying the principles

Do

  • Keep one clear primary action per view.
  • Make outcomes and next steps explicit.
  • Reuse verified tokens and existing components.
  • Design empty, loading, and error states up front.
  • Pair status color with text and an icon.

Don't

  • Add decorative gradients, shadows, or motion for emphasis.
  • Use marketing/SaaS visual language for a government tone.
  • Invent one-off spacing, colors, or shadow levels.
  • Overuse a single accent until hierarchy weakens.
  • Rely on color alone to convey meaning.
